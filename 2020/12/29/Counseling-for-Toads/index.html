Counseling for Toads

发表于 2020-12-29 | | 阅读数

Recently I read the book Counseling For Toads: A Psychological Adventure which uses characters from The Wind in the Willows(also wrote by Kenneth Grahame). The book shows readers a basic psychological counseling using accessibly language. The story in this book is about Toad, who once was a funny, extroverted guy but now staying at home all day long, even not willing to brush teeth. Therefore, toad’s friends brought him to go to counseling. Through ten counseling with heron, toad explored himself and find his confidence and hope back.

I conclude a few basic qualities that should owned by a psychological counselor and patients through reading the counseling meeting between Toad and Heron. I will also talk about the three psychological terms mentioned in this book and corresponding to my own life.

Basic qualities

For psychological counselor

Psychological counselor need to understand the meaning of patient but without too much empathy. Appropriate amount of empathy is necessary since empathy is a quite fragile balance. It is easy to get lost in too much negative emotions if experience patients’ negative emotions by oneself. A psychology counselor must be an individual who is calm, objective, rational and unified individual all the times. As a psychological counselor, protection of privacy and absolute neutrality are indispensable. Psychological counselors cannot judge patients who come to ask for help, no matter how heinous he is. A psychological counselor must treat every patient equally without and blame or judge with own values. What you have to solve is patients’ problems, not patients themselves. Treat everyone with equal kindness and be their most faithful listener are essential.

For patients

For the patients, as mentioned in “Mr. Toad Going to See a Psychologist”, psychological counseling is a spontaneous process. The patient and the counselor are in a cooperative relationship. Only when patients want to counsel for themselves rather than to please others like their friends or family can be possible for patients and counselor to reach a consensus. The task of the counselor is not telling the patient what to do but lead the counselor to do self exploration instead. Like the toad in the book later, trying to be as positive as possible, work with the heron with a cooperative attitude, use the learning model to perceive own thoughts and feelings, and explore and self examine from more aspects.

Ego states

In “Mr. Toad Going to See a Psychologist”, there are mainly three kinds of ego state: Child ego state(p34), Parent ego state(p91) and Adult ego state(p111). The book says that the states can not be classify into positive or negative. These are only used to describe the actual state of a person, like Child ego state is only talking about the effect of being in Child ego state on people.
According to my own life experience, although not enough yet, I can already correspond some of my behavior to these three states.

Parent ego state

Parent ego state gives me kind of feeling like how superiors treat subordinates, a state of slightly superiority and authority. In our school House Sing-off competition in last month, I led bass practice and a boy gossiped a lot and often played on his phone. Since the rehearsal time was very tight and I was impatient, I criticized him and ordered him to give me his phone. At that time, I feel like in Parent ego state. Also, when I communicate with my dog, I usually use the phrases my mother often uses to talk to me without noticing.

Child ego state

Child ego state is very straightforward, that is, behaving like a child. When I am really upset or extremely happy, I can clearly recognize my Child ego state. When I am in a very excited mood, I am usually emotional and can be affected by my emotions easily in judgment and decision. I express my emotions without thinking too much when I get along with my best friends, and that is when my Child ego state often appears the most. But actually I am not able to clearly indicate whether I am in Child ego state when I get along with my parents. Maybe it is because Child ego state is originally derived from the experience of getting along with my parents so I just follow it unawarely?

Adult ego state

I cannot easily recognize my Adult ego state. However, when I was writing the paragraphs above and trying to find out my ego states, I might be in the state of Adult ego state, because when analyzing the ego states, I was thinking rationally and corresponding to the knowledge I had learned, making connections but not thinking about the judgment and suggestions given by my parents. Maybe when I am planning before traveling, I am also in Adult ego state. For instance, I need to think logically about whether the hotel are suitable, the temperature, the weather, what kind of clothes are needed and what time should we depart in order to avoid crowds, etc.

Parent ego state is more likely harm others unconsciously whereas Child ego state is easy to be harmed as children’s emotions and mind is not as mature as adults. Adult ego state is the most objective state and the most suitable state for people to make vital decisions.

In all

The book says that “some concepts generated during the consultation process may seem stupid, illogical, and even scary at first. But the more you can help you deepen the concept of yourself, the more likely it is to cause intense resistance. Concepts are the easiest to break our psychological balance”. In normal life, some behavior seems just like “how it should be done” and “it has always been like this.” But looking back carefully, you will find that this kind of behavior is likely to be “reasonable” caused by your own positioning and judgment. Similar to PLOM (whole term: Poor Little Old Me. p88) and NIGYYSOB (whole term: Now I Get You, You Son Of Bitch) games, it can be incredible for people to classify their behavior into these two games: I’m so poor; they are all such stupid. In fact, they may be the reasons of the truest thoughts and behaviors in my heart.
